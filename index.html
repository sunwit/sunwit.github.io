I received my PhD from Department of Computer Science and Engineering in the Ohio State University, Columbus, Ohio, USA in 2022

I develope innovative sensing systems for real-world applications in human health, safety, privacy, and sustainability.

My research is driven by curiosity to explore science and technology and solve challenging technical and societal problems in real-world settings. I work across machine learning, signal processing, hardware, and optimization to design and build intelligent systems for real-world applications. I have made multiple inventions including safe vehicle-pavement and vehicle-vehicle interaction, ubiquitous and accessible computing tools for human health on dysphagia, healthy diapering, and human behavior understanding like stalking vehicle detection and human activity recognition, ubiquitous wireless connectivity, and location privacy protection for WiFi users.
